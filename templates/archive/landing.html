What is a Rorschach Test?

Through asking people to express what they see in inkblot cards, they often show more of themselves than their conscious selves may have intended. This can lead to better insights into the underlying motivations of the person’s issues and behaviors.

How to use this test?

You are shown a series of inkblot cards. Select the one you’re drawn to the most. Look at this card for about thirty seconds. What does the inkblot look like? The symbols you see might reveal details about your personality.

Historical background

A Rorschach Test, also known as Rorschach Inkblot Test or just Inkblot Test, is a psychological evaluation in which a person’s perceptions of inkblots are analyzed using psychological interpretation.
The original test was developed in 1921 and is named after its author, the Swiss Freudian psychiatrist and psychoanalyst, Hermann Rorschach.
Original Herman Rorschach inkblots, CC0 Public Domain
As a child he loved to express himself creatively through painting and drawing. He was known to his friends as ‘inkblot’ since he enjoyed creating inkblot pictures.
The flourishing psychoanalysis, that became popular during the early 20th century, constantly reminded him of his childhood inkblots. While still in university, he began showing inkblots to patients and analyzing their responses.
Later on he wrote his book Psychodiagnostik, which laid the foundation of the Rorschach test. In the 1960s, it became a widely used test in mental health facilities.
Although criticisms of the original test system have been disproved, some researchers continue to raise questions. That includes the testers objectivity, the scaling bias with great numbers of responses and the accuracy of diagnoses.
Since over 100 years the Rorschach Test helps people to understand their own thoughts and wishes.
